Who We Are

Students Defender is on mission to end harassment, in all its forms, in Pakistan Colleges and universities. We work together to understand the problem, ignite public debate, and develop innovative strategies that result in safe and welcoming environments for all.

What We Do

Our mission is to end harassment in all its forms by transforming the culture that perpetuates harassment and discrimination against vulnerable groups specially women. We build environment where students have the freedom to move through public space, participate in civic life, and reach their full potential.

How We Work

We train young leaders to become Students Defender on their campuses by providing them opportunities like; ongoing training and support. We immediately intervene with the help of hundreads of Lawyers around the country for free legal assistance.

Welcome to Students Defender

Preventing and effectively addressing sexual harassment of women in colleges and universities in Pakistan is a significant challenge. A systemwide change to the culture and climate in our nation’s colleges and universities can stop the pattern of harassing behavior from impacting the next generation of women entering science, engineering, and medicine.
Female harassment in Pakistan is becoming more common in universities, as thousands of harassment cases have been reported in universities. Teachers in the universities have unlimited authority and use their powers to exploit students, especially female ones.
According to Dukhtar foundation, a non-government organization, they have received more than 20,741 complaints, astonishingly, 16981 complaints – 82% of the total – were filed by female students of different universities. The complaints received from the universities are regarding professors/lecturers’ sexually harassing female students.
In some cases, the university harassment committee members were also found involved in harassing students, so normally the female students hesitates while approaching the university harassment committee as they usually protect their colleagues.
Kashmala Tariq, Federal Ombudsman for Protection against Harassment, while sharing her views in an event, she said that a major cause of harassment in universities is the semester system which empowers the teachers to exercise unlimited influence.
Whether you are facing Sexual Harassment, Sexual Assault, Hazing or Abusive Behavior or Sexual Misconduct or unjustified disciplinary action by your campus, you have the right to defend yourself and protect your future.
Sudents Defender work extensively with young people, specially students, through trainings and sensitization campaigns that help develop a stronger identity and the confidence in them. Our approach to mass media campaigns across the country to change behaviours and attitudes, and create a cultural shift and make Students Harassment unacceptable. Students Defender will also provide free legal assistance to vicitms of sexual harassement throught a network of hundreas of lawyers across the country.

Common Harassment

Harassment is a form of discrimination that consists of three types of harassing behavior: (1) Gender Harassment (verbal and nonverbal behaviors that convey hostility, objectification, exclusion, or second-class status about members of one gender); (2) Unwanted Sexual Attention (unwelcome verbal or physical sexual advances, which can include assault); and (3) Sexual Coercion (when favorable professional or educational treatment is conditioned on sexual activity).
